How It Works

BEFORE START

Setup

The broker (admin) registers all 10 teams, assigns starting cash (‚Çπ5,00,000 each) and distributes equal shares of every stock. The presentation screen goes live on the projector showing all stock prices and the leaderboard.

SESSION 1 ‚Äî 15 MIN

News drops. Markets open.

Flash news appears on screen for each stock ‚Äî earnings reports, debt updates, margin changes. Stock prices begin drifting based on news sentiment. Teams read the news, analyze the impact, and start planning their moves.

DURING SESSION

Negotiate & Trade

Teams talk to each other, negotiate prices, and agree on deals. Once a buyer and seller agree on a stock, quantity, and price, they come to the broker together. The broker enters the trade and it executes instantly ‚Äî shares and cash transfer between teams.

SESSION 2 ‚Äî 15 MIN

New news. New opportunities.

A fresh round of news drops for every stock. Prices shift again. Teams that positioned well in Session 1 may profit ‚Äî or face new headwinds. More trading, more negotiation.

SESSION 3 ‚Äî 15 MIN

Final session. Last chance.

The final round of news. This is your last opportunity to trade. Prices settle. Teams make their final moves to maximize portfolio value before the market closes.

FINAL

Leaderboard decides the winners

After Session 3 closes, all stock prices are final. Each team's net worth is calculated: cash + (shares √ó current price). The leaderboard ranks all 10 teams. Top teams win.

04

How to Trade

You don't trade on a screen ‚Äî you trade by talking. Here's the process:

Read the news

Watch the big screen. When flash news drops for a stock, decide if it's good or bad. Think about which stocks to buy or sell.

Find a trade partner

Walk around. Talk to other teams. If you want to buy TCS, find a team willing to sell TCS. If you want to dump CEAT, find a buyer.

Negotiate the price

You and the other team agree on a price and quantity. The market price on screen is a reference ‚Äî you can trade above or below it. It's your negotiation.

Go to the broker

Both teams come to the broker's desk together. Tell the broker: who's buying, who's selling, which stock, how many shares, and at what price.

Trade executes instantly

The broker enters it into the system. Cash moves from buyer to seller. Shares move from seller to buyer. The trade shows up on the big screen and the leaderboard updates in real-time.

üí° Pro Tips Buy stocks with positive news before the price rises too much. Sell stocks with negative news before they fall further. Pay attention to what other teams are doing ‚Äî if everyone wants the same stock, the price goes up. Think about supply and demand, not just the news.
05

Rules

üìå Core Rules All trades must go through the broker ‚Äî no side deals. Both the buyer and seller must be present at the broker's desk. You can only sell shares you actually own (no short selling). You can only buy if you have enough cash. The broker's execution is final.
‚õî Not Allowed No trading between sessions (only during the 15-minute market windows). No colluding to manipulate prices across multiple teams. No accessing the admin/broker system. No harassment or unsportsmanlike conduct.

Net Worth Calculation

Your net worth = your remaining cash + (number of shares in each stock √ó that stock's current market price). This updates live on the leaderboard.

Price Impact

When trades execute, they can slightly move the market price depending on the trade size and the stock's liquidity. Big trades on small stocks = bigger price swings.

Strategy Hints

There's no single winning strategy. Here are a few approaches teams have used:

üì∞ News Trader

ü§ù The Negotiator
